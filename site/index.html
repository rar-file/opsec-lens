How it works

A swarm of agents, working the image like an investigator

Every step runs on gemma-4-31b on Cerebras and streams to the UI live, so you watch the deduction happen.

1
Read every detail
EXIF GPS check, then zoom/OCR spotters read every sign, plate, brand and house number in the frame.
2
Six recon lenses + sun & shadow
Environment, built environment, text/language, culture, infrastructure — plus solar geometry that leaks your latitude and the time of day.
3
Hypothesise & debate
Candidate locations are ranked, then a prosecutor and a skeptic argue each one before the verdict.
4
Pinpoint & verify
Triangulate landmarks via OpenStreetMap, then capture real Google Street View and have Gemma confirm it's the same place.
5
OPSEC leak report
A calibrated location plus a severity-ranked list of what every clue gives away — and exactly how to scrub it.
What it surfaces

The “how did it know that” receipts

Same place, confirmed

Your photo set side-by-side with the real Street View it matched, with a visual-match score.

Calibrated precision

Confidence mapped honestly onto a street → city → region → country ladder, with the radius it really supports.

Cue-stack meter

How many independent location cues corroborate the guess. Stack ~5 and you're usually inside a few kilometres.

Sun & shadow chronolocation

What the shadows give away about your latitude band, the season and the time of day.

Adversarial debate

A prosecutor and a skeptic argue every candidate, so the verdict isn't one model's lucky guess.

OPSEC leak report

Every leak, ranked by severity, with a concrete scrub checklist — strip EXIF, blur plates, downscale, crop the skyline.

Defensive by design

Point it at your own photos.

The same capability that makes this unsettling is exactly why you should run it on yourself first. OPSEC Lens shows you what an attacker could already work out — so you can fix it before you post. It is a privacy tool, not a tracking one.
